Taekwondo is a sport that is played at international level. It is also played in olympics. But when you play taekwondo at international level. There are rules to follow. The objective of playing taekwondo is to land kicks and punches on the body either on torso or face. The sparring takes place between two competitors. There is a red corner for one competitor and a blue corner for other competitor. The kicks and punches should be accurate and powerful to gain points. After three rounds, whoever scores more points becomes the winner.
Some of the rulesfollowed in taekwondo are:

Dress code Rules

  1. All competitors must be wearing dobok i.e., the official dress of Taekwondo during the competition.
  2. All coaches must be in tracksuits during the competition
  3. All officials must be in dark navy blue pants and tie along with white shirt during the competition

Sparrinng Rules

  1. All competitors must wear their equipments properly. This includes gloves, head gear, chest guard, boots, mouthguard and groinguard.
  2. All fighters should fight in their own age group and weight category. Not following this can lead to elimination from the tournament.
  3. 3 points are given for jumping kicks hitting the face.
  4. 2 points are given for jumping kicks hitting the torso and standing kicks on the face.
  5. 1 points are given for punches on face and standing kicks on torso.
  6. 2 rounds of 2 mins are held and if there is a tie 3rd round comprises of 1 min.

Sparring Fouls

  1. No holding
  2. No pushing
  3. No kicks below the waist
  4. No kicks allowed on the back
  5. No use of illegal tools or weapons
  6. Nails should not be long.
  7. No face punches allowed for 10 years or younger.
