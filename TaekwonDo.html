TAEKWONDO AND IT'S ORIGIN

WHAT IS TAEKWONDO

Taekwondo is a form of martial art in which "TAE" means Kick, "KWON" means Punch and "DO" means art. It is one of the most modern form of martial arts. It comprises of three things kicks, punches and patterns/tuls. Taekwondo mainly uses techniques to fight with the opponent. It uses head high kicks and punches on face and mainly focuses on self-defense. The oath and tenets of taekwondo is memorized by every student. The oath shows the diligence and the student's motication towards taekwondo with humanity included in it.

HISTORY OF TAEKWONDO

Although the history of martial arts is still a mystery. Modern Taekwondo differs from other martial arts. Trchnically, 1955 marks the starting of taekwondo as an art in Korea. During that year, a leading board was formed which included of masters in the art, instructors and prominent leaders of society. After a good discussion, on 11th April, the board decided on the name TaeKwon-Do. In 1959, Taekwondo entered in international countries and people started getting themselves engaged in martial arts. Many of these black belt holders such as Nam Tae Hi, President of the Asia Taekwon‑Do Federation; Colonel Ko Jae Chun, the 5th Chief of Taekwon‑Do instructors in Vietnam; Colonel Baek Joon Gi, the 2nd Chief instructor in Vietnam; Brigadier Gen. Woo Jong Lim; Mr. Han Cha Kyo, the Head Instructor in Singapore and Mr. Cha Soo Young were a part of making Taekwondo an international sport. In 1965, Genral choi retired and due to disputes with the korean government, General choi was exiled out of the country. He started promoting Taekwondo in other countries like Austria, Vienna and more. In 1966, March 22nd International Taekwondo Federation was formed with the involvement of countries like Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore, West Germany, the United States, Turkey, Italy, Arab Republic of Egypt and Korea.

OATH OF TAEKWONDO

The oath goes as follows:
1. I shall observe the tenets of Taekwon-Do.
2. I shall never misuse Taekwon-Do.
3. I shall be a champion of freedom and justice.
4. I shall respect the instructor and seniors.
5. I shall build a more peaceful world.

TENETS IN TAEKWONDO

The tenets of Taekwon-Do are as follows:

1. INDOMIDABLE SPIRIT
To have indomitable spirit means to have the courage to stand up for what you believe in,no matter what odds you are up against, and to always give 100% effort in whatever you do.

2. COURTESY
Showing courtesy to all, respecting others, having manners as well as maintaining the appropriate etiquette at all times, both within and outside the dojang.

3. INTEGRITY
Although it may be similar, this form of integrity takes on a wider role than defined in the common dictionary. In Taekwondo, integrity means not only to determine what is right or wrong but also having the conscience to feel guilt if one has done wrong and to have the integrity stand up for what is right.

4. PERSEVARANCE
One will persevere time and time again until they have achieved a result which is adequate towards what one was trying to achieve.

5. SELF-CONTROL
This means to not only have control over one's physical acts, but also their mental thoughts and actions.
